Toronto Traffic Counts (Midblock)

This site explores City of Toronto midblock traffic studies short term counts (typically 1 day to 1–2 weeks) collected at road segments across the city. Counts go back to the early 1990s and are used to inform safety, traffic calming, and street design decisions. (Source: City of Toronto)

  • By Location: browse a street segment and see weekly averages and weekday patterns.
  • By Year: view study results grouped by study year.
  • By Type: filter by the kind of study (volume only, speed+volume, classification).

Context: The City reports that motor vehicle count studies span from the early 1990s onward and are used for initiatives like speed limit changes and traffic calming. The specific Open Data catalogue entry for “Traffic volumes, midblock vehicle speed, volume and classification counts” is currently marked as “Retired.”

What the Columns Mean

Column Meaning
_id Unique row identifier for the Open Data database.
count_id Unique identifier for each study; cross-references 15-minute observations in svc_raw_data.
count_type Type of data collected: ATR_VOLUME (volume only), ATR_SPEED_VOLUME (speed+volume), VEHICLE_CLASS (vehicle class + volume).
count_date_start / count_date_end Start/end date of the study period.
count_duration Study length in hours.
location_name Readable site name (e.g., “Bloor St: Yonge St – Park Rd”).
longitude / latitude Site coordinates.
centreline_id Identifier to join with Toronto Centreline geometry for mapping.
avg_daily_vol Average daily vehicle volume over the study duration (grand total ÷ number of study days).
avg_weekday_daily_vol Average weekday daily volume (weekdays only). May be none for weekend only studies.
avg_weekend_daily_vol Average weekend daily volume (Sat/Sun only). Present for studies that include weekends.
avg_wkdy_am_peak_start / avg_wkdy_am_peak_vol Weekday AM peak hour start and average vehicles during that hour.
avg_wkdy_pm_peak_start / avg_wkdy_pm_peak_vol Weekday PM peak hour start and average vehicles during that hour.
avg_speed Mean vehicle speed over the study duration (if speed data collected).
avg_85th_percentile_speed / avg_95th_percentile_speed Speed at/below which 85% / 95% of vehicles travel (if available).
avg_heavy_pct Percent of vehicles that are heavy (buses + trucks, FHWA classes 4–13), if classification collected.

How to Read the Charts

  • Weekly Average: we group study days by ISO week (strftime('%Y-%W')) to smooth single-day/short-run studies.
  • Day-of-Week: average daily volume by weekday (Sun–Sat) to show typical weekly pattern.
  • Notes: Studies are short-term snapshots (not continuous counters). Some locations have only 1–2 study weeks, so lines may connect widely spaced points in time.
